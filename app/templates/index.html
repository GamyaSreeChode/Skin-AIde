What are Skin Diseases?

Skin diseases are conditions that affect your skin. These diseases may cause rashes, inflammation, itchiness or other skin changes. Some skin conditions may be genetic, while lifestyle factors may cause others. Skin disease treatment may include medications, creams or ointments, or lifestyle changes.

Skin Diseases We Detect

Actinic Keratoses

Precancerous rough patches caused by sun damage. Can develop into skin cancer if untreated.

Basal Cell Carcinoma

The most common type of skin cancer. Appears as pearly or waxy bumps on sun-exposed skin.

Benign Keratosis

Non-cancerous skin growths like seborrheic keratosis and solar lentigo.

Dermatofibroma

Harmless skin nodules usually caused by minor injury or insect bites.

Melanoma

The most dangerous form of skin cancer. Early detection is critical.

Melanocytic Nevus

Common mole. Usually harmless but must be monitored for changes.

Vascular Lesion

Abnormal blood vessel growth such as angiomas or hemangiomas.
